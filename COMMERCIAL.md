# Commercial licensing

This project is licensed under [PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0](LICENSE) for non-commercial use. **Any commercial use requires a separate paid license.**

## What counts as commercial use?

Per the PolyForm Noncommercial license, anything that isn't a non-commercial purpose. Concretely, you need a commercial license if you:

- Deploy this software inside a for-profit company (including for internal tools)
- Use this software to deliver paid services or consulting work
- Host it as part of a paid SaaS product
- Embed it in a commercial product (boxed or downloadable)
- Use it to generate revenue, directly or indirectly
- Use it on behalf of a paying client (agency / contractor work)
- Use any output generated by this software (the `llms.txt` files it produces) on a commercial website, where "commercial" means the website's primary purpose includes revenue generation

## What's still free under the non-commercial license?

The license is permissive for:

- **Personal use**: hobby projects, learning, study, side projects, personal websites
- **Research**: academic research, papers, public-knowledge contributions
- **Education**: classroom use, tutorials, course materials, student projects
- **Charity / non-profit**: charitable organizations, public-safety, environmental, religious, government
- **Evaluation**: trying out the software to decide whether to license it commercially

If you're not sure which category you fall into, ask.

## How to get a commercial license

Email the maintainer with:

1. Your company name and website
2. Approximate intended scale (single internal deployment / multi-tenant SaaS / consulting deliverable / etc.)
3. Whether you need the plugin layer, the A2A server, the cookbook, or all three
4. Any specific use case details (the more you share, the faster the conversation)

**Contact**: open a private GitHub issue or use the contact link on https://github.com/ali-saadat — the maintainer will reply with a quote.

## Pricing

Pricing is set per-deployment and per-use-case. There is no fixed price list because the right number depends heavily on:

- Whether it's a one-off use vs ongoing service
- Whether it's wrapped in your own paid product
- Scale (1 deployment vs many)
- Industry vertical

Expect a conversation, not a checkout page. The maintainer is happy to discuss reasonable terms — the goal of the dual-license structure is to keep the work freely available for the people it most benefits (researchers, hobbyists, non-profits) while ensuring commercial users contribute back.

## Why a non-commercial license, not MIT?

Earlier versions of this repository (commits up to and including `5eb4ae4`) were MIT-licensed. The license was changed because:

1. The plugin embeds substantial original research (three-axis empirical baseline, 17 anti-patterns, 18 sectors, 13 languages, the SOTA refinement pipeline) that took significant labor to produce.
2. MIT lets large commercial users adopt this work freely while small contributors get no compensation. The dual-license model ensures commercial value flows back to the maintainer.
3. Non-commercial use — the vast majority of real users (hobbyists, researchers, educators, non-profits) — remains exactly as free as it was under MIT.

The license change applies to new code from commit `5eb4ae5` forward. Code at or before commit `5eb4ae4` remains MIT in its frozen historical form (you can clone an earlier tag if you specifically need MIT-licensed snapshots — though most users will want the latest version with the refinement pipeline).

## What if I just go ahead and use it commercially without a license?

Don't. The PolyForm Noncommercial license is a recognized, well-drafted legal instrument. Violation entitles the licensor to revoke your license and pursue remedies. The "first violation grace period" in the license (32 days to come into compliance) is the maintainer's only soft-touch concession — repeat or willful violations end your license immediately.

The maintainer would much rather have a quick licensing conversation than enforcement action. The licensing conversation is normally easy.

## Contributing

By contributing code via pull request, you agree that your contribution can be made available under both the PolyForm Noncommercial license (for the public repo) and any commercial license the maintainer grants. The maintainer will not accept contributions that come with their own license restrictions inconsistent with this dual model.
