# Français (fr) — French

French business communication conventions. France tends toward more formal communication than English-speaking countries; Quebec French has some differences in tone.

## Formality calibration

| Context | Formality | Form of address |
|---|---|---|
| Close colleagues | Casual: "Bonjour [Prénom]," | Informal `tu` |
| Cross-functional thread | Professional: "Bonjour [Prénom]," or "Bonjour [Madame/Monsieur]," | Formal `vous` (default in business) |
| Senior / management | "Bonjour Madame/Monsieur [Nom]," | Formal `vous` |
| External / vendor | "Madame, Monsieur," (when unknown) or "Madame/Monsieur [Nom]," | Fully formal `vous` |
| Quebec / Canadian French | Often slightly less formal | More `tu` use possible |

## Honorifics

- **Monsieur** — Mr.
- **Madame** — Mrs./Ms.
- **Mademoiselle** — archaic; do not use in business
- Multiple titles: `Madame la Directrice`, `Monsieur le Président` (in very formal contexts)

## Formal vous vs informal tu

In French business, `vous` is default. Wait for explicit invitation ("on peut se tutoyer") to switch to `tu`. Premature `tu` is awkward.

In tech startups (especially in Paris, less so in Lyon/regions), `tu` is more common. In traditional industries (finance, legal, government), `vous` is universal.

## Common business email phrases

| Purpose | Français |
|---|---|
| Opening thanks | "Merci pour votre relecture attentive et vos retours" |
| Acknowledging concern | "Je souhaite aborder ce point directement" |
| Presenting findings | "Voici les résultats de nos vérifications:" |
| Honest framing | "Je souhaite être transparent sur la signification du succès dans ce contexte" |
| Citing evidence | "Selon l'étude SE Ranking (n=300 000 domaines, novembre 2025)..." |
| Closing with offer | "Je reste à votre disposition pour tout échange" |
| Polite disagreement | "Je vois les choses légèrement différemment, car..." |

## Closing patterns

| Formality | Closing |
|---|---|
| Standard formal | "Cordialement," |
| Slightly warmer | "Bien cordialement," |
| Most formal | "Je vous prie d'agréer, Madame/Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées," (very formal, rarely used in tech) |
| Casual professional | "Bien à vous," |
| Quebec context | "Salutations," |
| Internal `tu` | "À bientôt," |

## Honest-expectations opening (translated)

> "Avant de continuer, je souhaite poser des attentes réalistes sur ce que llms.txt fera et ne fera pas — car trois études indépendantes (SE Ranking n=300k, OtterlyAI 90 jours de logs, Search Engine Land test sur 10 sites) n'ont relevé aucune augmentation mesurable des citations IA."

## Three-reasons framing

> "Trois raisons qui résistent à l'examen: (1) remplacer un fichier existant cassé est indiscutablement mieux, (2) le fichier sert également de base interne pour notre propre travail IA, (3) la compatibilité future ne coûte rien. La justification est défensive, pas offensive."

## Date and number formats

- Date: `15/05/2026` (DD/MM/YYYY)
- Number: `1 234,56` (space as thousands, comma as decimal) — France standard
- Number: `1 234,56` — Quebec generally same
- Currency: `1 234,56 €` (France), `1 234,56 $` (Quebec)
- Time: 24-hour clock standard (`14h30`)

## Cultural notes

- **Opening salutation matters** — "Bonjour" without name is acceptable; "Salut" is too casual for business
- **Closing must match opening tone** — if you opened with "Madame, Monsieur," you close with formal
- **GDPR / CNIL awareness is high** — French stakeholders are particularly attentive to privacy
- **Quebec French**: more anglicism-tolerant in tech contexts; "courriel" (email) vs "email" — both acceptable
- **Switzerland / Belgium French**: largely similar to France conventions
- **AI-specific terms**: "intelligence artificielle" (IA) — full phrase common in formal contexts
