# English (en)

The universal default for llms.txt advisor communication. Full templates in `../../stakeholder/email-templates-en.md`.

## Formality calibration

| Context | Formality |
|---|---|
| Internal team, established working relationship | Casual: "Hi [Name]," — first names — "Best" / "Cheers" close |
| Cross-functional, first contact | Professional: "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name]," — "Best Regards" close |
| External / vendor / leadership | Formal: "Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name]," — "Sincerely" or "Best Regards" close |
| Public communication | Brand voice as defined |

US vs UK conventions:
- US tends toward more casual openings ("Hi") even in business
- UK retains slightly more formal patterns ("Dear Mark," with comma)
- US "Sincerely" vs UK "Yours faithfully" / "Yours sincerely"

## Common business email phrases for llms.txt context

| Purpose | Phrase |
|---|---|
| Opening with appreciation | "Thank you for the careful review and the specific examples" |
| Acknowledging concern | "I want to address this directly" |
| Presenting findings | "Here's what we found:" / "The verification results below" |
| Honest framing | "I want to be honest about what success means here" |
| Citing empirical evidence | "Per the SE Ranking study (n=300,000 domains, Nov 2025)..." |
| Closing with offer | "Happy to jump on a call" / "Let me know how you'd like to proceed" |
| Polite disagreement | "I see this slightly differently" / "Worth flagging that..." |

## Specific patterns for llms.txt advisory

### Honest-expectations opening

> "Before we continue, I want to set realistic expectations on what llms.txt will and won't do — because three independent studies (SE Ranking n=300k, OtterlyAI 90-day logs, Search Engine Land 10-site test) all found no measurable AI-citation lift, and I'd rather we ship with the right expectations than feel disappointed six months from now."

### Three-reasons-that-survive-scrutiny framing

> "Three reasons survive scrutiny: (1) replacing a broken existing file is unambiguously better, (2) the file doubles as internal grounding for our own AI work, (3) forward-compatibility insurance costs us nothing. The case is defensive, not offensive."

### Vendor pushback

> "Appreciate the outreach. Before we engage, I'd like to understand the empirical basis for your approach. The independent studies I'm aware of found no measurable AI-citation lift. What's the evidence base behind your claims?"

### Encoding/quality dispute response

> "Thank you for the careful review and the specific examples — that lets us run a real verification. We ran [N] independent checks: [findings]. Our conclusion: [the source is clean / there's a real issue at X]."

| Tone | Closing |
|---|---|
| Standard professional | "Best Regards," |
| Slightly warmer | "Thanks," |
| Most formal | "Sincerely," |
| US tech casual | "Best," / "Cheers," |
| UK formal | "Yours sincerely," |

## Things NOT to say in English llms.txt communication

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "This will boost our AI citations" | Empirically unsupported |
| "All the major LLMs will read this" | False per empirical record |
| "This is the new robots.txt" | Misleading — different purpose, different standardization path |
| "We need this to stay competitive" | Vague; doesn't survive scrutiny |
| "Trust me" | Reduces credibility |
