# Español (es) — Spanish

Spanish business communication conventions. Note: there's meaningful variation between Spain (peninsular Spanish) and Latin American Spanish, especially in vocabulary and degree of formality.

## Formality calibration

| Context | Formality | Form of address |
|---|---|---|
| Close colleagues, same level | Casual: "Hola [Nombre]," | Informal `tú` |
| Cross-functional thread | Professional: "Hola [Nombre]," or "Buenos días [Nombre]," | Formal `usted` |
| Senior colleagues / management | "Estimado/a [Nombre]," | Formal `usted` |
| External / vendor | "Estimado/a Sr./Sra. [Apellido]," | Fully formal `usted` |
| Latin American context | Generally slightly more formal than Spain | Often default to `usted` |
| Spanish (peninsular) context | Faster transition to `tú` in tech | `tú` common after first email |

## Honorifics

- **Sr.** (Señor) — Mr.
- **Sra.** (Señora) — Mrs./Ms.
- **Srta.** (Señorita) — Miss (less used in modern business)
- **Don/Doña** — formal but rarely used in modern business email
- **Estimado/a** — "Esteemed" — gendered form (Estimado for male, Estimada for female)

## Common business email phrases

| Purpose | Español |
|---|---|
| Opening thanks | "Gracias por la cuidadosa revisión y los comentarios" |
| Acknowledging concern | "Quería abordar esto directamente" |
| Presenting findings | "Los resultados de nuestras verificaciones..." |
| Honest framing | "Quiero ser transparente sobre qué significa el éxito aquí" |
| Citing empirical evidence | "Según el estudio de SE Ranking (n=300.000 dominios, noviembre de 2025)..." |
| Closing with offer | "Quedo a su disposición para una llamada" / "Si tiene cualquier pregunta, no dude en contactarme" |
| Polite disagreement | "Lo veo ligeramente diferente porque..." |

## Closing patterns

| Formality | Closing |
|---|---|
| Standard formal | "Saludos cordiales," |
| Warm formal | "Un cordial saludo," |
| Most formal | "Reciba un cordial saludo," / "Atentamente," |
| Casual professional (LatAm) | "Saludos," |
| Casual professional (Spain) | "Un saludo," |

> "Tres razones que resisten el escrutinio: (1) reemplazar un archivo existente roto es claramente mejor, (2) el archivo sirve también como fundamento interno para nuestro propio trabajo de IA, (3) la compatibilidad futura no tiene coste. El caso es defensivo, no ofensivo."

## Date and number formats

- Date: `15/05/2026` (DD/MM/YYYY) — Spain and most LatAm
- Number: `1.234,56` (period as thousands, comma as decimal) — Spain
- Number: `1,234.56` (US format) — Mexico, some LatAm
- Currency: `1.234,56 €` (Spain), `€1,234.56` or `1.234,56 €` (varies LatAm)

## Cultural notes

- Spanish business email is often more elaborate than English — the opening and closing carry more weight
- "Quedo a su disposición" ("I remain at your disposal") is standard closing context, not over-formal
- Time-zone-aware greetings: "Buenos días" (morning), "Buenas tardes" (afternoon/evening) more common than just "Hola" in formal contexts
- Regional terms: "ordenador" (Spain) vs "computadora" (LatAm) — be aware
