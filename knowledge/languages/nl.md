# Nederlands (nl) ‚Äî Dutch

Dutch business communication conventions. Dutch culture values directness, egalitarianism, and pragmatism. Formality is relatively flat compared to German or Italian.

## Formality calibration

| Context | Formality | Form of address |
|---|---|---|
| Close colleagues | Casual: "Hoi [Voornaam]," | Informal `je`/`jij` |
| Cross-functional, established thread | Professional: "Hallo [Voornaam]," | Often informal `je` (Dutch tech is `je`-default) |
| Senior management / executive | "Geachte [Heer/Mevrouw] [Achternaam]," | Formal `u` |
| External / vendor / first contact | "Geachte Heer/Mevrouw [Achternaam]," | Formal `u` |
| Belgium (Flemish) | Generally slightly more formal than Netherlands | More `u` use |

## Honorifics

- **Heer** ‚Äî Mr.
- **Mevrouw** ‚Äî Mrs./Ms.
- **Dhr.** ‚Äî abbreviated Heer
- **Mevr.** ‚Äî abbreviated Mevrouw
- **Dr.** ‚Äî Doctor (used in academic / medical contexts)
- **Mr.** ‚Äî Meester (Master, used for lawyers ‚Äî note: NOT the English Mr.)
- **Ir.** ‚Äî Ingenieur (Engineer; used with last name in formal context)

## Formal "u" vs informal "je"

Netherlands defaults to `je` faster than most European languages. In tech and startup culture, `je` is universal from the first email. Banking, law, government remain `u`-default.

| Informal `je` | Formal `u` | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `jij hebt gezien` | `u heeft gezien` | you saw |
| `jouw voorstel` | `uw voorstel` | your suggestion |
| `kun je` | `kunt u` | can you |
| `jou` | `u` | you (object) |

## Common business email phrases

| Purpose | Nederlands |
|---|---|
| Opening thanks | "Dank u/je voor de zorgvuldige review en feedback" |
| Acknowledging concern | "Ik wil dit punt direct adresseren" |
| Presenting findings | "Hieronder de resultaten van onze verificatie:" |
| Honest framing | "Ik wil transparant zijn over wat succes in deze context betekent" |
| Citing evidence | "Volgens de SE Ranking-studie (n=300.000 domeinen, november 2025)..." |
| Closing offer | "Voor vragen sta ik tot uw/je beschikking" |
| Polite disagreement | "Ik zie dit iets anders, want..." |

## Closing patterns

| Formality | Closing |
|---|---|
| Standard formal | "Met vriendelijke groet," |
| Slightly warmer | "Vriendelijke groet," |
| Casual professional | "Groet," / "Groetjes," |
| Most formal | "Hoogachtend," (rare in modern business) |
| `je`-context | "Mvg," (Met vriendelijke groet, abbreviated) |

## Date and number formats

- Date: `15-05-2026` or `15 mei 2026`
- Number: `1.234,56` (period as thousands, comma as decimal)
- Currency: `‚Ç¨ 1.234,56`
- Time: 24-hour clock standard

## Cultural notes

- **Directness is highly valued** ‚Äî Dutch business communication is unusually direct by European standards
- **Egalitarianism** ‚Äî hierarchical deference is minimal; speaking up across levels is expected
- **Pragmatism over ceremony** ‚Äî concise emails preferred; no need for elaborate openings
- **GDPR awareness is high** ‚Äî Dutch Data Protection Authority (AP) is active
- **English is widely used** ‚Äî Dutch business professionals often switch to English seamlessly, especially in tech
- **Belgian Flemish vs Dutch Dutch** ‚Äî largely similar but some vocabulary differs
- **AVG = GDPR** ‚Äî Dutch acronym for the same regulation
