## Names вАФ patronymic matters

Russian names: First name + Patronymic + Last name (e.g., `–Ш–≤–∞–љ –Я–µ—В—А–Њ–≤–Є—З –°–Љ–Є—А–љ–Њ–≤`).

In formal business address, use **first name + patronymic** вАФ NOT first name alone, NOT last name alone:
- Formal: `–Ш–≤–∞–љ –Я–µ—В—А–Њ–≤–Є—З` вАФ first name + patronymic
- Most formal: `–≥–Њ—Б–њ–Њ–і–Є–љ –°–Љ–Є—А–љ–Њ–≤` вАФ Mr. + last name
- Informal (close colleagues): `–Ш–≤–∞–љ`

The patronymic (–Я–µ—В—А–Њ–≤–Є—З for son of –Я—С—В—А) is a sign of respect; using it shows you know how Russian business culture works.

## Honorifics

- **–У–Њ—Б–њ–Њ–і–Є–љ (gospodin)** вАФ Mr.
- **–У–Њ—Б–њ–Њ–ґ–∞ (gospozha)** вАФ Mrs./Ms.
- **–£–≤–∞–ґ–∞–µ–Љ—Л–є/–£–≤–∞–ґ–∞–µ–Љ–∞—П (uvazhayemyi/uvazhayemaya)** вАФ "Respected" вАФ gendered formal opener
- **–Ф–Њ–Ї—В–Њ—А (doktor)** вАФ Doctor (academic)
- **–Я—А–Њ—Д–µ—Б—Б–Њ—А (professor)** вАФ Professor

The Soviet-era "–Ґ–Њ–≤–∞—А–Є—Й" (Comrade) is NOT used in modern business вАФ only in historical/ironic contexts.

## Formal "–≤—Л" vs informal "—В—Л"

`–Т—Л` (capitalized in formal letters) is default in business. The transition to `—В—Л` typically requires explicit invitation or significant working relationship.

In capitalized form (`–Т—Л`, `–Т–∞—Б`, `–Т–∞–Љ`) in correspondence вАФ capitalization itself shows formal respect.

## Closing patterns

| Formality | Closing |
|---|---|
| Standard formal | "–° —Г–≤–∞–ґ–µ–љ–Є–µ–Љ," (S uvazheniyem вАФ "With respect") |

## Date and number formats

- Date: `15.05.2026` (DD.MM.YYYY)
- Number: `1 234,56` (space as thousands, comma as decimal)
- Currency: `1 234,56 вВљ` (rubles) or `1 234,56 —А—Г–±.`
- Time: 24-hour clock standard

## Cultural notes

- **Hierarchical respect** вАФ copy senior colleagues; address most senior person first
- **Patronymic = respect signal** вАФ using it correctly is appreciated
- **Direct + polite combination** вАФ Russian directness with formal politeness markers is the norm
- **Avoid excessive small talk** in business emails; get to the point efficiently
- **Holiday awareness** вАФ Russian New Year holidays (Jan 1-8) and May holidays affect availability
- **Cyrillic script** вАФ ensure UTF-8 handling for Cyrillic characters
- **Sanctions context** вАФ many Russian business communications now route through Eurasia/CIS partners; be aware of sanctioned-entity concerns

## Russian-specific llms.txt concerns

- **Federal Law 152-FZ (Personal Data Law)** вАФ Russia's data protection regime
- **Roskomnadzor compliance** вАФ for sites operating in/serving Russia
- **Yandex** is dominant in Russian search вАФ Yandex Webmaster Tools matter more than Google for Russian sites
- **Cyrillic URL handling** вАФ modern sites typically use Latin slugs, but some legacy Cyrillic URLs exist
