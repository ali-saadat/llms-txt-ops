# License model

> [!important] Dual-license (v2.0.0 onward)
> **Non-commercial use**: free under PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0.
> **Commercial use**: requires a separate paid license.

## What counts as commercial use

❌ Requires commercial license:
- Deployment inside a for-profit company (incl. internal tools)
- Paid services / consulting deliverables
- SaaS / hosted-product use
- Embedded in a commercial product
- Use that generates revenue, directly or indirectly

✅ Free under PolyForm Noncommercial:
- Personal hobby projects, learning, side projects
- Academic research, papers, classroom use
- Charity / non-profit / public-safety / government
- Evaluation (deciding whether to license)

## License history

| Period | License |
|---|---|
| Initial release through commit `5eb4ae4` (v1.x) | MIT |
| From commit `32e4afc` onward (v2.0.0+) | **PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0** |

> [!note] Forward-only
> Code at commit `5eb4ae4` or earlier remains MIT in its frozen historical form. Anyone who cloned before the change keeps their MIT rights to that snapshot.

## How to obtain a commercial license

Open a private GitHub issue or contact the maintainer via the GitHub profile. See `../COMMERCIAL.md`.

## Why the change

> [!quote] From COMMERCIAL.md
> The plugin embeds substantial original research (three-axis empirical baseline, 17 anti-patterns, 18 sectors, 13 languages, the SOTA refinement pipeline) that took significant labor. MIT lets large commercial users adopt this work freely with no compensation flowing back. The dual-license model ensures commercial value contributes back while the work stays free for the 90%+ of users (researchers, hobbyists, educators, non-profits) it most benefits.
